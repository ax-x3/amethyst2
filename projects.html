UD Grinding Calculator 2.0
For you grass-avoiding nerds who spend every available moment speeding down I-76, here's a tool to simplify your calculations. Find out just how many miles you need to reach a rank or credit goal. (Yes, this also works with French cars.)




stupidCalculator
This is the world's worst JavaScript calculator. Designed to take command line-esque input, it can perform any of the four basic operations: multiplication, division, subtraction, and concatenation. Just give it a minute, it'll finish processing soon.




stupidCalendar
If you ever need the time from a random timezone or the date measured in days of June, this calendar is for you! It even features an immersive "dark" mode! Simply hit the button, and you'll feel your eyes relax instantly.




calculator_
This is one of my most ambitious projects. It's a full graphing calculator entirely from scratch, on Scratch. Totals to 4,000+ lines of blockcode. It's designed to look like and function similarly to Desmos.




raycasting test
A simple raycasting experiment to draw a 2-dimensional level map in 3D. Built on Scratch. Use [left] and [right] to look around and WASD to move.

Note: This project is extremely unoptimized and may slow down some devices. Be sure to press the red Stop button before moving on.




Vegetable Samurai
Welcome to Vegetable Samurai, the totally-not-a-knockoff version of Fruit Ninja. Click or press space while dragging the cursor to cut vegetables on the cutting board. Avoid cutting onions, they'll make you cry. Don't let non-onion vegetables fall off of the board uncut, that would be wasting food. You can gain lives by getting streaks of 10.




Mandelbrot Fractal Rendering
The Mandelbrot Set: one of the most recognized mathematical fractals, rendered in Scratch. Once upon a time, I knew how the math worked. Now, the secrets of rendering are etched in blockcode. Check it out for yourself and tell me how it works if you manage to figure it out.
